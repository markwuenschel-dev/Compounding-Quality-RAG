## Purpose

Define how synthetic inquiries use available customer-provided context, storage information, dosing descriptions, and administration details.

## Scope

This SOP applies to palatability concerns, oral-liquid shortage reports, smell/appearance/viscosity concerns, possible administration problems, apparent efficacy concerns, and customer reviews with narrative text.

## Review Procedure

1. Review the concern narrative for customer-provided details.
2. Identify whether the customer describes administration method, storage conditions, timing of the concern, vomiting, foaming, refusal, leakage, damaged packaging, or broken dosage forms.
3. Identify missing information needed for the next review step.
4. Do not assume access to longitudinal patient history unless the synthetic record explicitly provides prior-context information.
5. Treat global escalation triggers as escalation issues rather than palatability-specific logic.

## Palatability and Flavor Rejection

Routine palatability concerns may include refusal, foaming, taste aversion, or attempts to cleanse the palate. Severe vomiting may move the concern toward suspected ADE review.

Hospitalization, death, legal threat, and veterinarian allegation of harm are not palatability-specific criteria. They are global escalation triggers handled under the escalation SOP.

## Oral Liquid Shortage Context

Routine oral-liquid shortage questions are usually handled through frontline counseling unless the narrative includes leakage, package damage, compounding-record discrepancy, or temperature excursion.

## Notes and Limitations

This SOP uses only customer-provided or synthetic review-summary context. It does not imply access to real order history, patient records, or customer account records.
