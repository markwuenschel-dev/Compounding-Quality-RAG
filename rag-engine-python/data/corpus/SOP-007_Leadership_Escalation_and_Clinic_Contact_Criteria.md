# Leadership Escalation and Clinic Contact Criteria

## Purpose

This synthetic SOP defines global escalation triggers for the public Compounding Quality RAG project.

This document is synthetic and is not real company policy.

## Global Escalation Triggers

The following triggers override routine handling: pet death, pet hospitalization, threatened legal action, veterinarian allegation of harm from the compound, possible contamination, wrong patient or wrong medication, repeat issue same lot or batch with concerning conditions, and rare regulatory or compliance concern.

## Risk Lane

If a global escalation trigger is present, use `life_threatening_or_legal`.

This risk lane may be appropriate even before all facts are known when the reported concern could involve severe harm, legal exposure, wrong medication, or contamination.

## Handling Path

When global escalation triggers are present, use `leadership_escalation_before_resolution` or another escalation-oriented handling path supported by the synthetic case.

Do not route these cases as routine counseling, document-only handling, or frontline delegation.

## Veterinarian Allegation of Harm

If a veterinarian alleges harm from the compound, treat the case as escalation-relevant even if causality is not confirmed.

The assistant should say alleged or reported unless the synthetic evidence confirms the event.

## Hospitalization or Death

Hospitalization or death after exposure should be handled conservatively.

The assistant must not claim causality. It may state that the report triggers escalation review because of severity.

## Wrong Patient or Wrong Medication

Wrong patient or wrong medication concerns should generally be treated as life-threatening or legal risk until review clarifies what happened.

Severity depends on the medication, species, dose, route, whether the medication was dispensed or delivered, and whether it was administered.

## Possible Contamination

Possible contamination should trigger escalation-oriented handling and review before routine resolution.

## Refusal and Limitation Rules

The assistant must not state that leadership was contacted unless supplied in a synthetic review summary.

The assistant may say escalation is supported or that escalation review is indicated by the synthetic SOP.
