# Customer Context and Administration Review

## Purpose

This synthetic SOP defines how customer-provided context, storage, dosing, administration, palatability, and shortage details should be considered.

This document is synthetic and is not real company policy.

## Customer Context

Customer context may include what the customer observed, when the issue occurred, whether the medication was administered, whether symptoms occurred, whether the veterinarian was contacted, storage conditions, administration method, prior tolerance, and whether review text is present.

Customer context helps determine missing information, risk lane, handling path, and resolution options.

## Customer Review With Text

A low-star customer review with text should be reviewed for quality or safety signals.

If review text suggests harm, product defect, wrong medication, contamination, leaking, missing item, or device failure, route according to the relevant SOP.

If the review text describes routine dissatisfaction without quality or safety signals, outreach or documentation may be appropriate depending on the synthetic review summary.

## Customer Review Without Text

A low-star review without text may support record/context review and documentation if no quality concern is found.

Customer outreach is generally not expected when there is no text to clarify, unless other available synthetic evidence identifies a quality or safety concern.

## Palatability and Flavor Rejection

Routine flavor refusal without vomiting, injury, hospitalization, death, contamination, wrong medication, legal threat, or veterinarian allegation of harm is usually expected self-limiting.

Possible outputs include counseling or follow-up, administration guidance, alternate flavor or dosage form discussion, and documentation after quality review.

Flavor refusal alone does not automatically require refund, reship, or leadership escalation.

## Administration Technique

Some concerns may be influenced by administration technique, including foaming, refusal, oral-liquid shortage perception, or difficulty using a device.

The assistant may identify administration technique as missing information. It must not assume the customer administered the medication incorrectly.

## Storage Context

Storage context may matter for temperature excursions, BUD questions, formulation concerns, odor changes, color changes, or suspected efficacy concerns.

If product-specific stability or real BUD is requested and unsupported, refuse or list the needed synthetic fields.

## Oral Liquid Shortage Context

For oral liquid shortage concerns, review whether the case describes true missing volume, leakage, package damage, administration difficulty, spillage, directions or quantity misunderstanding, or compounding or dispensing discrepancy.

Routine shortage counseling may be delegated back only when there is no product-quality signal.

## Refusal and Limitation Rules

The assistant should not claim customer history, real review history, or contact outcomes unless supplied in the synthetic record.

The assistant should clearly separate what the customer reported from what the reviewer concluded.
